About the Project

Preventing amputations through evidence-based, cost-effective foot care

Diabetic foot complications, including ulcers, infections and amputations, represent one of the most devastating and costly consequences of diabetes. In Belgium, thousands of patients face lower limb amputations each year, many of which are preventable with the right interventions.

The PARADISE project (Towards Pressure-Adherence & Education Driven Service in Diabetic Foot Disease ) takes an interdisciplinary approach. By combining biomechanical analysis with health economic evaluation, we identify the most effective and cost-efficient strategies for diabetic foot care in Belgian clinical practice.

About PARADISE

What does PARADISE stand for?

Pressure-Adherence & Education Research And Diabetic foot disease — Integrated Service & Education

This PhD project is funded and hosted at KU Leuven Campus Bruges. It combines quantitative biomechanical assessment with health economic modelling to identify the most effective and cost-efficient care pathways for patients at risk of diabetic foot complications.

The project runs in close collaboration with Belgian diabetic foot clinics and builds directly on existing Belgian consensus guidelines developed in partnership with the BDFPG.

Instrumentation

Data Collection Instruments

The PARADISE project relies on three validated measurement systems to objectively assess physical activity, adherence and plantar pressure in patients with diabetic foot pathology.

Activity Monitoring

Orthotimer

The Orthotimer is a small, lightweight data logger designed to monitor footwear adherence. It is typically embedded in therapeutic footwear or insoles to record the duration of footwear wear.

Within the PARADISE project, the Orthotimer is used to objectively quantify how long patients actually wear their prescribed custom made foot orthosis — a key determinant of healing outcomes that is often poorly captured by self-report alone.

Inertial Measurement

McRoberts DynaPort

The McRoberts DynaPort is a validated wearable accelerometer system worn at the lower back that provides detailed classification of postures (sitting, standing, lying) and transitions, as well as step counts and walking bout characteristics during free-living conditions.

In the PARADISE project, the DynaPort is used to obtain objective, 24-hour ambulatory profiles of patients. This allows us to characterise real-world mobility patterns and quantify physical activity levels that relate to both ulcer healing and quality of life outcomes.

Plantar Pressure Analysis

Tekscan F-Scan Go

The Tekscan F-Scan Go is a portable, in-shoe plantar pressure measurement system that uses thin, flexible sensor insoles to capture dynamic pressure distribution across the entire plantar surface during walking. It provides spatial pressure maps, peak pressure values, pressure-time integrals and force data in real time.

In the PARADISE project, the F-Scan Go is central to our biomechanical assessment. It enables identification of high-risk pressure regions, evaluation of offloading footwear effectiveness and longitudinal tracking of pressure changes over the course of treatment.
